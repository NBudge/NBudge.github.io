Worldbuilding

To create a fantasy world for DnD, you need to create a fantasy world. This can be done in two ways, depending on your personal ability and skill: outwards or inwards.

Do you create a world and fill it with all sorts of villains, heroes, nations, landmarks, and locations first? That is an example of inwards worldbuilding, you set the scale of your artwork and then paint in finer and finer detail (but not too much or the paint will just dribble off the canvas and stain your reputation).

Alternatively, you can focus solely on the exploits of your players and generate the world as you go, adding places, towns, cities, and NPCs as you go. This allows for great freedom in allowing PCs to do whatever they want but requires you to constantly be ready to create content on the fly. I like to use a balance of these two approaches, creating a world and filling it in as the game progresses, but hey, you do what feels most comfortable to you.
